Your electrical panel is the heart of your home's electrical system. Every circuit, outlet, and appliance depends on it. In Orlando's humid, storm-prone climate — with many homes built in the 1970s and 80s — panels age faster than you might think. Recognizing the warning signs early can prevent outages, fires, and costly damage.

## 5 Warning Signs You Need a Panel Upgrade

**1. Breakers trip frequently.**
If you find yourself resetting the breaker box more than once a month, your panel may be undersized or worn out. Repeated tripping indicates the panel can't handle the electrical load of modern appliances.

**2. Lights flicker or dim when appliances turn on.**
Noticing your lights dim when the AC kicks in or the refrigerator cycles? That's a classic symptom of an overloaded panel or loose connections inside it.

**3. You still have a 60-amp or 100-amp panel.**
Homes built before the mid-1990s often shipped with 60 to 100-amp service. Today's homes — with central AC, dishwashers, electric ovens, and growing numbers of EV chargers — need 200-amp service at minimum.

**4. You're adding an EV charger.**
Level 2 EV chargers draw 30-50 amps on a dedicated 240V circuit. Older panels often can't accommodate this without a full upgrade. We see this come up weekly in Orlando as more homeowners go electric.

**5. You're renovating or adding to your home.**
New kitchens, master bath additions, or converted garages all add significant load. Permits and inspections typically require a panel upgrade if you're adding more than a circuit or two.

## Why Orlando Homes Need Panel Upgrades

Florida's climate isn't kind to electrical equipment. High humidity accelerates corrosion inside panel boxes — bus bars, breakers, and neutral connections deteriorate faster here than in drier regions. Combined with salt air near the coast and summer thunderstorms that can surge through old panels, Orlando homes are particularly vulnerable.

Many neighborhoods — College Park, Winter Park, Audubon Park, Conway — have homes built 40-60 years ago with original panels still in service. Federal Pacific, Zinsco, and Challenger panels in particular have documented safety issues and should be replaced on sight.

Add to this the rapid EV adoption in Orlando: Tesla, Rivian, Ford F-150 Lightning — our streets are filling up. Every one of those drivers eventually needs home charging, which often means a panel upgrade.
