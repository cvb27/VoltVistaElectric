Here's what Orlando homeowners need to know before installing an EV charger.

## Level 1 vs Level 2 Home EV Chargers

**Level 1** charging uses a standard 120V household outlet. Every EV ships with a Level 1 cord. It's free to install (just plug in), but painfully slow — adding just 3-5 miles of range per hour. An overnight charge may only give you 40-50 miles. Fine for short commutes; not practical for most drivers.

**Level 2** uses a dedicated 240V circuit, the same voltage as a clothes dryer or oven. Charging speed jumps to 25-40 miles of range per hour — a full overnight refill for most vehicles. This is what the vast majority of EV owners want at home.

Level 2 charging is faster, more convenient, and typically adds value to your home when it's time to sell.

## What Does EV Charger Installation Involve?

1. **Panel inspection.** We first check if your electrical panel can handle a new 240V circuit. Many Orlando homes have 100-amp panels that were fine for gas appliances but don't have headroom for a 40-amp EV charger.

2. **Dedicated circuit install.** A Level 2 charger requires its own 240V circuit, typically 40-50 amps with a dedicated breaker. The wire run from the panel to the charger location is the biggest variable in cost.

3. **Permit and inspection.** Orange County and the City of Orlando require a permit for any 240V circuit work. We handle the permit application and coordinate the inspection.

4. **Charger mounting and commissioning.** Once the circuit is live, we mount the charger on the wall, connect it to the circuit, and test it with your vehicle.

## How Much Does It Cost in Orlando?

A typical Level 2 EV charger installation in Orlando ranges **$800–$2,500** all-in, depending on:

- **Distance from panel to charger location.** A garage adjacent to the panel is cheapest. A detached garage or charger on the opposite side of the house adds labor and wire cost.
- **Panel capacity.** If your panel needs upgrading first, add $1,500–$3,500 for that work.
- **Permit and inspection fees.** Usually $75–$200 depending on municipality.
- **Charger model.** Basic chargers start around $400 (Grizzl-E). Smart networked chargers with apps run $600–$1,000 (Tesla Wall Connector, ChargePoint Home Flex, JuiceBox).

Most Orlando installs without a panel upgrade land in the $1,000–$1,500 range.
